Quantitative Game Simulations

Authors: Ian Fong | Institution: New York University

A collection of quantitative finance and game theory projects exploring optimal strategies, probability theory, and statistical simulation in competitive gaming scenarios.

Card Drawing Game Strategy

Probability • Game Theory • Statistics • Strategy
Analysis of optimal strategies for a competitive card guessing game with 11 players and a standard 52-card deck. Explores probability-based decision making and expected value calculations to maximize winning chances in a winner-takes-all scenario.
In Progress

Poker Tournament Simulation

Statistics • Game Theory • Logic • Strategy
Statistical simulation of multi-table poker tournament strategies, analyzing optimal betting patterns, stack management, and risk-reward calculations across different tournament phases and player counts.
Planned

Auction Bidding Strategy

Game Theory • Mathematics • Strategy • Logic
Mathematical modeling of optimal bidding strategies in various auction formats including English, Dutch, and sealed-bid auctions. Incorporates psychological factors and information asymmetry in competitive bidding scenarios.
Planned

Combinatorial Optimization Game

Combinatorics • Algorithms • Mathematics • Logic
Exploration of combinatorial optimization problems in competitive settings, analyzing efficient algorithms for resource allocation, scheduling, and matching problems with multiple strategic players.
Planned

Stock Market Trading Game

Statistics • Algorithms • Probability • Strategy
Quantitative analysis of trading strategies in a simulated market environment with multiple participants. Explores algorithmic trading, market making, and statistical arbitrage in competitive financial markets.
Planned

Network Game Analysis

Game Theory • Algorithms • Mathematics • Logic
Analysis of strategic interactions on network structures, studying equilibrium concepts, coalition formation, and optimal strategies in graph-based competitive scenarios with information cascades.
