A comprehensive analysis of optimal strategies for a competitive card guessing game involving probability theory, game theory, and statistical simulation.

Problem Statement

Suppose you are playing a game against ten other opponents with a standard deck of 52 cards, where 26 cards are red and 26 cards are black. One by one, cards will be drawn from the top of the deck until the deck is empty. Before each card is drawn, you and your opponents will have the opportunity to guess whether the chosen card is either red or black. You will not know what guesses your opponents made – however, you will get to see the color of the card after it is drawn and know if your guess was correct or not. Whoever guesses the most amount of cards correctly will win $1,000 – there are no prizes for anyone besides the winner. In the case of ties, the winner will be chosen at random.

Objective: Describe a strategy to win the $1,000 prize and estimate the success rate of this strategy.
